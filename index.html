Correlations Discovered Between smoking, access to healthcare and poverty.

A recent study examining US residents and the links between smoking, poverty and access to healthcare uncovered some surprising results.

The assumption that states with higher a percerntage of residents with access to healthcare would have lower rates of smoking was disproven. In fact, a graph charting states with the percentage of residents with access to healthcare and smoking rates revealed there may be a small positive correlation between the two. This demonstrates that states with higher percentages of residents with access to healthcare also have a slightly larger percentage of the population that smokes. Though perhaps the positive difference isn't statistically significant, it is clear there is not a negagive correlation, meaning the original assumption is incorrect.

Perhaps less surprising is that there is a stronger positive correlation between the percentage of residents living in poverty and the % of state residents that smoke. Smoking is a form of stress relief (among other possible uses), and poverty - along with other stressors associated with being poor - creates high levels of stress. A linear regression could be utlized to determine the extent to which these factors are positively correlated.
